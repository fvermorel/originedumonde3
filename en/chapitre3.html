Cronus the Tyrant
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Cronus succeeded his father as the king of the world; however, whilst more cunning than Uranus, he was just as brutal a tyrant. The Cyclops, those skillful blacksmiths* (blacksmiths create metallic things and weapons by heating them in their furnaces), had provided him with the weapon that enabled him to seize power. If they decided to serve somebody else, Cronus would be in danger. So he hurled them into Tartarus, the deepest prison of the underworld. A warning to the other titans who would dare defy his authority! He took as a wife his sister Rhea, the goddess of nature and fertility.
Titans and gods often got married to their brothers or sisters, which would be strictly forbidden to humans.

A God who Swallowed his Children
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
But Cronus was afraid of suffering the same fate as his father Uranus and being driven from power by one of his children. So whenever Rhea had a baby, he swallowed it at once. Like her mother Gaia before her, Rhea rapidly became very angry. Had she been freed from Uranus’s tyranny only to see her children devoured right from birth? When she gave birth to Zeus, her last born, she decided to replace the baby with a stone. Cronus did not see the difference and swallowed the stone.

Zeus’s Youth
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Rhea found a hiding place for Zeus in Crete, and entrusted him to the nymph* Adrastea (nymphs are immortal creatures linked with nature) and the she-goat Amalthea, which fed him from its teats, together with his foster brother, the god Pan. Bees provided Zeus with honey, enabling him to grow in size and strength. All those creatures were taking risks, for Cronus was still reigning over the universe. What danger for them if the god of time had found Zeus! But thanks to their courage and devotion, his empire would soon end.

When he became a young adult god at the height of his powers, Zeus met Metis, the goddess of intelligence. She had also fled Cronus, who wanted to make her his mistress. Not eager to share Rhea’s fate, she had used her visionary powers to find a means of putting an end to his reign of terror. This was how she discovered the existence of Zeus, to whom she suggested a plan.

The Gods Escape
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Zeus mixed an emetic in the wine cup of Cronus, who started to vomit at once. He spat out his five children Hestia, Hades, Poseidon, Hera, and Demeter, who immediately joined Zeus. In a last belch, he brought up the stone Rhea had made him eat. Frightened at having been betrayed, he ran out of the palace and called the titans who were still faithful to him for help. It was the beginning of the first and most terrible war the universe had ever known. Could the world resist it ?
Vocabulary : an emetic is a medicine that helps to make you vomit. Cronus had not really eaten his children the way we understand it. In fact, he had enclosed them in his stomach. In freeing them, Zeus became their chief.
