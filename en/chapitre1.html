Our World
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Do you recognize this place? The air is so pure that if you were to inhale the fragrance of the lavender, your lungs would sting. Far away, waves are lapping on a luminous and smiling sea where dolphins are playing. The spring morning is so dazzling that you think it will never end. It is your world, it is our world, our Earth, the way we have always known it. But did you know that the world has not always been like this?
Original Chaos
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
At the beginning was Chaos. Where did it come from? How did it start? No answer can be given, for neither time nor space existed then. Chaos was all shapes and all substances, perpetual movement and agitation. An unlimited and untidy mass filling the whole universe: that was Chaos, the beginning and the end of everything.
Vocabulary: Today, we call “chaos” a confusing state where you cannot find things. If your room is not tidied up your mother will probably tell you “it is chaos”!
The Sky and the Earth
 ⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Chaos gave birth to Eros, the force of attraction, then to Gaia, the Earth. For the first time, a firm, solid and eternal plinth emerged from the original disorder. Then came Uranus, the Sky, followed by the Night and the Day. The universe finally got organized. But I’m sure you would agree with me: it would have been very difficult for us humans to live and inhabit such a place.
In ancient times the earth was believed to be flat and the sky to cover it. It was only in the 17th century that Galileo demonstrated that our planet was round and that it revolved around the sun (see below, the “Clever Corner”)...

Uranus’s Sovereignty
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Uranus now governed the universe. From the original Chaos he conserved brutality and arbitrariness* (somebody is said to be arbitrary when he does whatever he wants without worrying about others). He covered Gaia completely and clung to her to make her have children. Thus were born the first Titans. But Uranus did not allow them to come out into the world: they remained squeezed in Gaia’s womb, underneath the Earth, which caused her terrible suffering.
