Earth and Sky

The earth

The earth, our planet, is not flat as was believed by most of the people before us. It is a sphere that revolves around the sun. Its diameter is more than 40,000 kilometers and two thirds of its surface is below water. She is roughly 4.6 billion years old.

The moon

The moon revolves around the Earth in the same fashion that the sun revolves around the sun. Scientists think that she was born very long ago when another planet impacted earth and in doing so broke apart a chunk. She is illuminated by the reflection of the sun and it is for this reason that she changes shape nearly each day.

The Sun

The sun is an immense ball of 1,300,000 kilometers in diameter. On its surface, the temperature is close to 6000 degree Celsius, but its core is even hotter at more than 16,000,000 degree Celsius. The sun is more than 5 billion years old. When, in another 5 billion years it finishes to combust, it will explode and the Earth will disappear with him.

The other Stars

There are many other stars like the sun. Some are even larger, some much smaller. Today, scientists look for those likely to have in their orbits planets comparable to the Earth: perhaps life has developed there also, at many light years from us.

Galaxies

Our Sun is one of two billion stars which together make up the “Milky Way”. But this galaxy is not alone: there exist millions of others in all kinds of shapes and sizes. Amongst all this immensity, our planet Earth, is but a small grain of dust!

The steps to the creation of the Earth

The Big Bang

Currently scientists believe that our universe was born with the Big Bang: an immense explosion that happened 14 billion years ago. Very rapidly thereafter, matter quickly spread and created the first stars and the first galaxies.

The creation of the earth

5 billion years ago, the Earth was just a great cloud of burning gas that turned around the sun. With the time, the gases solidified and cooled down. Luckily, the gases formed an atmosphere without which life would have never existed.

The beginnings of life

Once cooled, the planet was covered with immense oceans and there was no firm land. It is in water that life was first born: first single cell microscopic organism, and then more complex ones, plants and first water animals… When continents appeared, they progressively learned to get out of the water onto land and walk.

Dinosaurs

Around 230 years before Jesus Christ, the first dinosaurs appeared: they were immense creatures which ruled without equals on the planets for dozens of millions of years. Around 65 million years before Jesus Christ, they all disappeared, for a reason that not yet fully understood.

The beginnings of humanity

About 2.5 million years ago, the first prehistoric men appeared. But they not look much like us and still have the appearance of great apes. Our species, home sapiens is born around 200,000 years ago. His nearest cousin, Neanderthal man disappeared around 50,000.

The end of the Ice Age

Around 12,000, it’s the end of the ice age. The earth is warming up and the condition of life for humans improves. While the prehistoric men were nomads and lived from hunting and fishing, some people first starting settling and invented agriculture. They then build the first cities: it is the beginning of the world as we know it today.

The creation of the world in other civilizations

The civilizations of the Book

For the Christians, The Muslim and the Jews, it is God that created the world: the first day he created light, the second day he created the sky. The third day he separates the sky from the earth. The Fourth day it is the sun and moon. The Fifth day, it is the turn of the animals and on the six he creates man in his own image.

"Le jardin d'Eden" from Brueghel

The Egyptians

At the beginning, there was a great ocean by the name of Nu. The sun is born from there and birthed Shu, the god of wind and Tefnut, the moisture. From there he created the sky and the earth which at their turns had many children: Isis and Osiris, who like Zeus and Hera, who are the god couple who govern the universe.

The woman character is the sky

The Hindus

The Hindus believe in an egg that divides itself. It is for this reason that the world, universe is one common thing and indissociable. The three principal deities of Hinduism are Brahma who is the creation, Vishnu who is the preservation and Shiva who is the destruction necessary to the future rebirth.

The Nordic People

At the beginning of the world, there is nothing but nothingness, fire and flames. The first living thing is a giant Ymir who falls asleep and during his sleep birthed numerous giants. One of them, Odin, killed him and with his body created the world: with his flesh he made the earth, with his blood he made the sea and his cranium was used to create the celestial sphere and his teeth and bones became rocks.

"Ymir killed by Odin", by Lorenz Frølich (1820-1908)

The Dogon People

For the Dogons also, all begins with an egg: Amma the creator of all thing lives within it until he creates words with his saliva and then the first seed. Amma, then, creates two twins, male and female who resemble fish. Finally the rain falls and with it begins the human era.

Toltec Religions

The religions of the Toltecs were those of the ancient civilizations of South America. At the beginning, Tonacatecuhtli, the lord of our sustenance and Tonacacihuatl, lady nourishment, the first two gods. From their union is born a knife that breaks into shards when it falls. Of each shard a god is born. There is a multitude of them: some big, some small, some handsome, some ugly, some rich, some poor…
