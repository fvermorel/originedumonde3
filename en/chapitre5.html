The Reign of the Olympians
Zeus, King of the World
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
After their victory, the three divine brothers shared power. It was decided that the Earth should be the domain of all, and that everybody could wander there and act according to his desire. Zeus became the king of the gods and he built his palace at the top of Mount Olympus, from where he had commanded the war against the Titans. As the master of the thunderbolt, he was given the sky and the storms. And most of all, he became the supreme judge.
But what do the gods eat on their clouds? Neither bread nor meat, of course. In fact, they feed on nectar and ambrosia. These magic foods prevent them from getting old and make them immortal.
Zeus and Hera
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Zeus took as his spouse his sister Hera, the goddess of home and family. Contrary to his father and grandfather, he did not prevent his children from being born, provided that they showed him obedience. Thus were born the Olympian gods. The divine couple had to experience lots of turbulence: if Hera proved herself to be an honest and faithful spouse, it was not the same with Zeus. Whenever the king met a woman who pleased him, whether a goddess or a mortal, he fell in love with her. From these adventures countless heroes were born, whose stories will be narrated in our coming books.
Hera was not very pleased when Zeus was unfaithful to her. She did not hesitate to take revenge on her husband’s illegitimate children. You will know more about it when you read our book “The Twelve Labors of Heracles”.
I am boring... I am watching you.
The Kingdom of the Sea
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Poseidon got the kingdom of the sea. He governed the waves from his submarine palace and he divided the sea-foam on a cart drawn by dolphins. A whole joyful and turbulent people of undines, tritons, and nereids lived with him. But it was dangerous to offend him, as his anger could be immense and it was impossible to escape from him: with one blow of his trident he could overturn the Earth...
Poseidon is not a wicked god, but he does not tolerate any of his belongings been taken from him: you will have evidence of this when reading our book “Minos and Theseus”.
The Kingdom of the Dead
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The third brother, Hades, became king of Hell, the underworld kingdom where souls go after death. At the entrance he chained up Cerberus, a terrifying mastiff with three roaring heads. This was not to stop intruders from coming in, but rather to prevent the dead from going out! Then he entrusted Charon, his head servant, with the role of a ferryman. It was he who, aboard his boat, let souls cross the Styx and penetrate Hades’ kingdom in exchange for an obulus. He who came to him empty-handed could not cross the river, and roamed on the banks forever.
Though reigning over Hell, Hades is not a bad god. Generally he never intervenes in living people’s business.

Summer and Winter
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Although of a taciturn nature, Hades ended up feeling bored in his solitary kingdom. He came back up to Earth and met Persephone, his sister Demeter’s daughter. He fell in love with her and took her with him down to the underworld. Demeter, goddess of vegetation, was horribly upset by the loss of her daughter. She started to cry, cry, cry, and… nature wasted away. The grass and trees dried out, the fruit rotted, the flowers faded… Zeus intervened in order to find a compromise: Persephone would spend six months with her mother on Earth, and the six other months with Hades in Hell. That was the birth of the seasons: when Persephone is on earth, her mother Demeter is delighted, and it is spring: the vegetation becomes abundant and fruit grows. When she is in the underworld, Demeter cries, nature falls asleep, and it is winter.

An Empty World
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
This is our world. But rather empty and silent so far, isn’t it? That was the opinion of the gods who, to amuse themselves, decided to create animals: birds, bees, cows, sheep, fish, dogs, cats… oh, and to finish, one last little one. What is this creature without fur nor feathers, which moves awkwardly on two skinny legs? But… it’s you, it’s me, it’s man! Can you believe it? With this strange creature, a whole series of new stories will begin. Stories in which you and I are, so to speak, the heroes...
