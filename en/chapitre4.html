4.
The War of the Titans
The Battle Starts
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Cronus gathered his army of Titans on Mount Orthys. Zeus established the gods’ camp on top of Mount Olympus, the biggest mountain in the world, the summit of which touched the sky. Several Titans joined his camp, among them the brothers Epimetheus and Prometheus. However, the terrifying monsters coming from Uranus’s blood drops took sides with Cronus, and the gods could not win against them.
Mount Olympus is the highest mountain in Greece: its summit is 2,917 meters high. It should not be confused with Olympia, the town where the first Olympic Games were held!
Attack ! Send them back in Tartarus ! It won't be easy...
The Liberation of the Cyclops
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Up against a stronger enemy, the gods had to be the more cunning! Fortunately, Metis got an idea. She took Zeus up to the gates of Tartarus to free the Cyclops held prisoner by Cronus. Out of gratitude, they started to forge the weapons of victory. The Cyclops first created a helmet for Hades, which made him invisible and allowed him to attack his adversaries, who were thus unable to defend themselves. Then they forged a trident for Poseidon. When he hit the soil with it, it provoked earthquakes. Their last creation was the most powerful: it was the thunderbolt, and they offered it to Zeus.
For the Greeks, the thunderbolt came directly from Zeus: it was the manifestation of the anger of the gods’ king, so they were forced to take it seriously.
Free ! Thank you Zeus
Ten Years of War
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
With these weapons, the gods no longer feared the number nor the strength of the Titans. Moreover, the visionary powers of Themis and Prometheus enabled them to foresee their adversaries’ movements and to systematically surprise them in battles! The battle lasted ten long years before the gods had the upper hand: they hurled Cronus and his Titans into Tartarus and entrusted their guard to the Hecatonchires, the hundred-handed giants. But their rejoicing did not last long...
The Monster from Chaos
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Gaia remained neutral during the conflict, but when she saw her children defeated and imprisoned, she got furious. From the depths of her entrails, she freed the most terrifying monster: Typheus, the son of the original Chaos, whose fury and rage had no limits! Typheus, whose unique will was to destroy our universe! He piled up mountains on mountains to reach the level of the gods and to slaughter them. But Zeus threw his thunderbolt at the monster’s feet and made him fall down. Before he could get up, the gods emprisoned him under Etna where his fiery breath still terrifies humans...
The Victory of the Gods
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The gods had triumphed, and Gaia, sulking, shut herself up in eternal silence. Their power would never be contested from then on. It was the beginning of the golden age, an era of peace and happy harmony. But that world was not yet ours, and it had to change again before man could inhabit it.
Hurrah ! At last we won !
